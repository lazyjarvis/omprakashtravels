Andaman has a lot to offer, we're listing some of the selected tourist attractions and activities in Andaman

  • Cellular Jail

    Port Blair

    Cellular Jail, located at Port Blair, stood mute witness to the tortures meted out to the freedom fighters, who were incarcerated in this jail. The jail, completed in the year 1906 acquired the name ‘Cellular’ because it is entirely made up of individual cells for the solitary confinement.

  • Radhanagar Beach

    Havelock

    Crowned as “Asia’s best beach” and “7th best beach in the world” by Time Magazine. This beautiful sandy beache fringed with green canopy of the rain-fed forests beckons everyone to enjoy the frolic and fun at the azure sea.

  • Jolly Buoy

    Wandoor

    One place that must not be missed while you are going on a trip to Andaman is the Jolly Buoy Island. This island is located at a distance of 15 miles from the Wandoor Jetty. The underwater beauty of this place is divine. You will get to see many colorful fishes and corals. One can try out adventure sports like snorkeling.

  • Ross Island

    South Andaman

    This Island the erstwhile capital for the most of Andaman Islands from 1858 until an earthquake in 1941. Ross Island was also the base for the British Administrator of the penal colony in Port Blair. It now lies deserted, and the few signs of its colonial glory, such as the Chief Commissioner's house and the Presbyterian church, are dilapidated and overgrown.

  • Mud Volcano

    Baratang

    Watching a live volcano throwing out leaps of grey colored mud is a sight in itself. Isn’t it awesome that India is one among the few countries that have active mud volcanoes?

  • Bharatpur Beach

    Neil Island

    One of the finest and most spectacular beaches of Neil Island, Bharatpur Beach attracts a horde of tourists year-round. This beach has a sprawling stretch of sand and greenery that overlooks a vast expanse of the blue sea. What makes this seafront popular is the presence of colourful coral reefs, which fascinate visitors. These reefs are inhabited by a variety of beautiful aquatic creatures that can be seen swimming around.

  • Limestone Cave

    Baratang

    In this island, there are presense of huge deposits of Limestone in the form of caves. There are approximately 300 such caves of varying sizes in this area, of which the government has opened only one for the tourists.

  • North Bay

    South Andaman

    A journey of 15- 20 minutes from Jetty of Port Blair, takes you to the North Bay Island of Andaman. The beach here is renowned for its amenities and water sports like snorkeling and scuba diving.

  • Chidyatapu

    South Andaman

    Chidiya Tapu is located 25 km from Port Blair and is a tiny village in the southernmost tip of South Andaman. It is also known as the bird island. The region has a number of green mangroves and a lush forest cover. Birds are the major attraction here. There is a forest guest house at the top of the hillock that provides sunset views as well as views of the islands beyond Chidiya Tapu.

  • Elephant Beach

    Havelock

    Elephant Beach, located at a distance of around 10 km from the market, is one of the most popular beaches in Havelock Island for day trips. Reaching this beach is a hassle free affair as it is accessible either by 30 minutes hike on a jungle path or by opting for one hour boat ride. Known for its beautiful corals and exotic marine life, this beach is ideal for snorkelling.

  • Ross and Smith Islands

    Diglipur

    Like lovely tropical counterweights, the twin islands of Smith and Ross are connected by a narrow sandbar of dazzling white sand, and are up there with the best in the Andamans for both swimming and snorkelling.

  • Viper Island

    South Andaman

    Viper Island, named after the ship wreck event of a British trading ship named "Viper" in 19th century, is located near Port Blair. The ruins of an olden discarded jail, built by British in 1867, with yellow colored bricks and the gallows are seen in this Island. The Island was considered as the place of penal settlement for the freedom fighters of India.

  • Corbyn's Cove Beach

    Port Blair

    Secluded, and unspoilt, the Corbyn's Cove beach is just 8 kilometers from the City center, and a prominent sightseeing place in Andaman. With pleasant blue sea water and lush green coconut palms, the beach allows visitors to relax alongside and enjoy several water sports available on the shore of the beach.

  • Laxmanpur Beach

    Neil Island

    Laxmanpur Beach is located at a distance of around 2 km from Neil Jetty. This beach is situated in Laxmanpur village. It has white shell sand along with shallow water that makes it an ideal place for snorkelling. The beach is favourable for activities like sun bathing and swimming.

  • Naval Marine Museum : Samudrika

    Port Blair

    Samudrika Naval Marine Museum is a museum situated in Port Blair. The museum is run by the Indian Navy. This museum aims to generate awareness about the environment in the ocean and the marine life. The museum has five sections presenting history of Andaman Islands, Geographical information, people of Andaman, Archaeology and Marine life.

  • Chatham Saw Mill

    Chatham Island

    Established in 1836, one of Asia's largest saw mills was set up by the Britishers on Chatham Island. The mill is still functional even to this day. This is a good pit-stop for those who're interested in learning a little about the history and economy of the Andamans. It's a relatively off-beat experience, but worth your while—you'll get to see weathered machinery still trudging along.

  • Rajiv Gandhi Water-Sports Complex

    Port Blair

    These pristine islands offer some of fun loving unforgettable water sports activities. The facilities for these water sport activities are available in Port Blair and other islands. Andaman and Nicobar Islands, which is surrounded by rich coral reef eco system, offers unique life time diving experience.

  • Anthropological Museum

    Port Blair

    This museum provides a thorough and sympathetic portrait of the islands’ indigenous tribal communities. The glass display cases may be a tad old school, but they don’t feel anywhere near as ancient as the simple geometric patterns etched into a Jarawa chest guard, a skull left in a Sentinelese lean-to, or the totemic spirits represented by Nicobarese shamanic sculptures.

  • Mount Harriet

    Bambooflat

    Mount Harriet National Park (383 metres), is the third-highest peak in the Andaman and Nicobar archipelago next to Saddle Peak (732 metres) in North Andaman and Mount Thullier (568 metres) in Great Nicobar. The place is shown on the reverse side of Rupee 20 note in India. The park is named in commemoration of Harriet Tytler, the second wife of Robert Christopher Tytler, a British army officer.

  • Science Centre

    Port Blair

    It is a good place for both children and elders to spend time on. It provides us with information on various aspects.The science centre gives a detailed account about the ocean. The youngest rock sample in India can be seen here. The science centre is located close to the Corbyn’s clove beach. There is a planetarium in the centre which is portable and it is named as Taramandal.

  • Marina Park

    Port Blair

    Marina Park is a prime attraction in Andaman and Nicobar Islands. It is located in the unspoiled seashore close proximity to Water Sports Complex. The local inhabitants and the visiting tourists use this park for spending their valuable evenings by enjoying the nature’s gifts of pristine beauty, stunning sea waves and lush green vegetations.

  • Aquarium

    Port Blair

    The Fisheries Aquarium is located close to the Rajiv Gandhi Water Sports complex. The Aquarium is a praise worthy place enclosing many marine life including various kinds of sea fishes and turtles. It has the skeleton of a sperm whale, known for being the largest toothed whale.

  • Glass Boat Ride - Dolphin

    Port Blair

    Uncover the beauty of the underwater kingdom lying beneath the Andaman Sea, without getting your toes wet. Ride alongside Dolphins and watch their playful games. This boat provides a luxurious window into the mesmerizing world of corals and marine life of Port Blair, from within the comfort and safety of international quality speedboat.

  • Scuba Diving

    North Bay / Havelock

    An archipelago of more than 500 islands, The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are perhaps among the most exotic and remote destinations on the planet. Scuba Diving in Andaman are extremely popular, and you will find some of the world's best dive sites here.

  • Snorkeling

    Red Skin / Jolly Buoy / Havelock / Neil / North Bay

    Mesmerise yourself with the colourful and magical world that lies beneath the waters of Andaman. The snorkelling activity is carried out in most of the islands that allures with its crystal clear waters and live corals.

  • Semi Submarine Ride - Coral Safari

    Port Blair

    Coral Safari is a semi-submarine which will take you into the deep sea in a most comfortable 100 seater cabin, fully air-conditioned. When you watch the unbelievable under sea world through the large glass windows inclined at 45 degrees, it will give you a spine thrilling experience. People of all ages can comfortably enter and exit the vessel.

  • Trekking

    Mundapahad / Mount Harriet

    Andaman! Not just beaches, it's a trekkers' paradise too. With trekking trails that could take you from sunrise to sunset, lazing on the clear sands of the beaches is no longer the only option. Here are some amazing treks in the Andaman islands that will blow your mind. Mount Harriet, Mundapahad, Havelock-Elephant Beach, Baratang-Limestone caves are some of the popular trekking destinations in Andaman.

  • Sea Plane Ride

    Port Blair - Havelock

    Explore the Island Paradise from the sky with the India's first Seaplane service. Just climb into the seaplane, get a window seat and take off to enjoy aerial view of India's only active volcano, Barren Island, or fly over the magical sea to relish Havelock, Diglipur and Little Andaman Island and savour the lush green landscape from high above.

  • Helicopter Ride - Pawan Hans

    Port Blair - Havelock / Neil

    An experience of the lifetime to view the Andaman Islands from the top. Travel from South to North Andaman Islands in a Helicopter. Ride above beautiful coastline viewing dense tropical forests and the vast open seas. The trip takes you over some of the best destination in the islands within couple of hours. These choppers have a maximum capacity of 10 passengers (+2 crew).

  • Sea Walk

    North Bay / Havelock

    Sea Walking is one of the easiest ways to explore the underwater world at Havelock Island and North Bay Coral Island. Underwater Sea Walking is the safest underwater fun for the whole family. There is a constant supply of fresh air pumped into your helmet and your head remain dry, there is no restricting regulator or snorkel in your mouth to cause any feeling of discomfort or anxiety. The Underwater Sea Walk does not require any knowledge of swimming or diving and you are not even required to remove your glasses.

  • Cruise Ride - Markruzz / Coastal Cruise / Green Ocean

    Port Blair - Havelock / Neil

    M.V Makruzz / M.V Coastal Cruise / M.V Green Ocean are privately owned luxury catamarans in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. The state-of-the-art cruise services between Port Blair - Havelock / Neil are one of its kind in India. These vessels are enclosed entirely with picturesque windows which give a panoramic view of the entire sea and the flora and fauna of our beautiful islands.

  • Vehicle Ferry Ride

    Bambooflat / Middle Strait

    Ever wondered how we take our vehicles with us from one island to another? One of it's kind in India where you can enjoy your joyride with vehicles on board the same vessel.

  • Kayaking

    Havelock

    If you are an adventure freak, then be ready to get your hands on a couple of paddles and slip into a kayak . Kayaking options at Havelock include sea kayaking through the mangroves, and night kayaking where one can do gliding among bio-luminescence.

  • Sunbathing

    Havelock / Neil

    A journey to Andaman can never be complete without Sunbathing on sandy beaches. Sunbathing eases your mind as well as soul and sets you in gesture for the humdrum of life.

  • Beachside - Candle light Dinner

    Havelock

    Coming to Andaman on a honeymoon? If yes, make it a memorable one for a lifetime by having a Beachside - Candle light Dinner.

  • Night Stay - Beach Resort

    Havelock

    Include a Beach Resort stay in your travel list. These resorts are fenced with the stretch of white sandy beaches, interspersed with swaying palms, clear blue night sky and the gurgle of the sea. Sumptous interiors and superb modern facilities add to an atmosphere of discrete luxury.

  • Taste Sea Food - Lighthouse

    Port Blair

    The cuisine Of Andaman is like the island itself-tropical, exotic and refreshing. No wonder Andaman is an epicurean epicenter. Mainly famous seafood delicacies in Andaman are prawns, fish, lobsters, and crabs, which are easily available at the restaurants, and hotels.

  • Game Fishing

    Port Blair / Havelock / Neil

    Andaman is an ultimate heaven for fishing enthusiasts. It offers world-class sport fishing and angling. Black Marlin, Blue Marlin, Sailfish, Yellow fin Tuna, Dogtooth Tuna and Dorado have been caught in Andamans and its surrounding waters.

  • Luxury Yacht Ride

Travel with your loved ones and enjoy a night where no one else surrounds. Havelock Island

Havelock Island offers one of Asia’s best beaches, famous for its swimming elephants and unbeatable reef. Picture the white sandy beaches of Ko Pha Ngan before backpackers, full moon parties and internet cafés, and you have Havelock Island. For those searching for a peaceful escape, cut off from mobile phones, internet and commercial beach attractions, Havelock Island is certainly the next place to tick off your list. Note : There is no nightlife on the island.

Neil Island

Although its beaches are not as luxurious as its more famous island neighbour, Havelock, tranquil Neil has its own unique charm. There’s a wonderfully unhurried pace of life here; cycling through picturesque little villages is a stellar way to soak up the island's character. The main bazaar has a mellow vibe and is a popular gathering spot in the early evening. On Neil Island you’re about 40km from Port Blair, a short ferry ride from Havelock and several universes away from the bustle of life back home.

Baratang

Baratang is an island situated in middle Andaman and is known as the gateway to middle Andaman. The island is covered with rain forests and surrounded by mangroves. Baratang is home to the rare Limestone caves in India. The other main allures are the parrot islands, the blue beach at Baludera and the mud volcano. The resident and origin tribe of Jarawa’s also stir curiosity in every traveler.

Wandoor

The beautiful Wandoor is situated at Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park, around 30 Kms from Port Blair. For travelling to Jolly buoy and Red skin islands, people have to reach this place by road and board the ship. Nearly 15 islands are located surrounding this stretch of pristine coastline. The Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park stretches about 282 Sq.Km. Small ships start from this Wandoor Jetty towards Jollybuoy and Red Skin islands.

Cellular Jail

The Cellular Jail is the old colonial prison situated in Port Blair, the capital of Andaman and Nicobar islands. It was constructed by the Britishers during their colonial rule in India.The construction of the jail began in 1896 and completed in 1906, and was then used to imprison the independence leaders during the Independence movement in India. The jail complex is now owned by the Government of India and it is recognised as the national memorial monument showcasing the life of prisoners during the British period. The jail narrates of the horrifying and darkest period in the history of India.

Ross and Smith Islands

A boat ride of 25 minutes off Diglipur gets you to the serene twin islands called Ross and Smith. The beaches here promise uncluttered vistas and soft waves sweep up along the shore. The islands are actually separated by a narrow stretch of sand, technically making it a single beach.
